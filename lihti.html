The new design of the home page.

LIHTI

Internship experience focusing on graphics and web development. Gained insight on non-profit and start-up businesses.


Duration
Spring 2020 (January - Present)

Role
Researched and designed the Long Island High Tech Incubator's (LIHTI) website to reflect company's new marketing and branding strategy.

Skills
Product research, prototyping, cross-functional collaboration, design project management, and communication skills.

Tools
Figma, Illustrator


Overview
The Long Island High Tech Incubator is a non-profit organization that is dedicated to supporting early-stage and high-tech businesses by providing them with a number of resources and services.
Throughout the duration of the internship, my role included pushing forth the rebranding of the company through a series of new marketing initiatives. Some of those included implementing the new logo and branding elements, creating new physical promotional items and redesigning the current website.

Research

Focused on what about the design of the website lacked; which aspects lacked user engagement.

Looked into other incubator programs' websites/landing pages, such as 500 Startups and

MassChallenge. Their initiative was direct and the first thing the user sees on the main page. Further down the main page are statistics, representing the companies' reputable success and used as a marketing tactic. Branding and colors on their websites were simple and minimal.

Problem

The current website (left) did not align with the incubator's new branding and marketing. The design was outdated and did not stand out against other websites in the industry.

The information was also outdated and redundant, the design and content was last changed a few years ago. The website was difficult to navigate and find specific information.

While the mobile version of the site is configured, the accent colors are not consistent with the desktop version of the website or the brand itself at all.

Design

I created a high-fidelity user flow (right). This allowed me to be through with the design and help our team identify the errors earlier on, so that we could save time in the prototyping and development phase.

This flow clearly indicate how users would navigate the site. We aimed to prioritize the information that would be most valuable to a prospective applicant (first user profile) and then information that was simply documentation on the company's history and introductions to current tenant companies (second user profile).


Brand Consistency

The website needed to incorporate the colors schemes and fonts of the new branding.

To simplify and keep the typography cohesive, the fonts choices were 3 styles from the same font family, Montserrat.

Mapped user flow in initial designs, accounted for all the pages that were needed.

Changes

Minimized repetitive information by merging similar pages into one with multiple sections. Dropdown menus in header lists all available pages for easy navigation.

Updated colors and fonts of all pages to provide a more balanced and uniform look. Added more visuals and icons to engage users upon scrolling through home page. Also added statistics to home page to reflect company's reputable success and encourage early-stage businesses to apply to the incubator program.

Also updated mobile version to be more consistent with website's branding, reduced amount of text in mobile version as well.

Mobile version of the new design.

Takeaways

This internship experience allowed me to gain real experience with designing an interface. This was the first time I had worked on a larger scale project and while learning how to communicate with other designers and learning from them remotely. While unable to get a physical hands on experience, I gained fluency with online tools and became comfortable with design and prototyping tools.

This was the first time I was able to work on a full rebranding initiative of a company, as well. Through this I learned the importance of consistent branding and goal-driven marketing decisions. It aided in curating content that would target the ideal audience and recruit the appropriate clients.
